what is this webring and how does it work?

A webring is a collection of websites joined together in a circular structure. Unlike modern social feeds or directories governed by algorithms, a webring is a decentralized way to discover content. You navigate from one member site to another sequentially using the "prev", "next", or "random" links, exploring each person's unique space on the web directly.

This webring specifically connects student developers from all four VIT campuses—Vellore, Chennai, AP, and Bhopal. It functions as a directory of personal portfolios, blogs, and projects. When you click "next" or "prev", you are redirected to the next member's actual website. "Random" takes you to a random member's site, helping you stumble upon interesting projects and corners of the internet built by peers in our community.

If you are a student builder from VIT and maintain your own website, we'd love for you to join the ring. To get added, you can fill out our join form. Once approved, your site will be added to the circle, and you can add the navigation widget to your own homepage.
